Some Ground Rules

When you visit #8bitmooc, you are a guest in our home. We don't want your money, we just want to make sure that everyone behaves in a manner conducive to learning.

Rule one: Don't harass any of your classmates. This ranges from everything including sending them undesired messages, creating unnecessary requests for help, creating programs that are obscene or hateful, and so forth. Anyone doing so will be disciplined at the discretion of the site administrators, up to and including being banned from the website.

You own the rights to all of the code you submit to #8bitmooc, but understand that you also give us a non-exclusive right to use your code for non-commercial and research purposes. You are free to sell your code if you like, and we won't try to stop you. But we need to be able to analyze your code in order to conduct our research and improve #8bitmooc for everyone else. This is the price for taking our "free" course, and we feel it is a fair price.

Don't try to break the website. If you're interested in doing security testing, don't do us any favors - let us know first.

#8bitmooc is a safe space, committed to including everyone regardless of nationality, language, race, gender identity, age, or any other category. We want everyone to feel welcome, and if someone is making learning unpleasant for you, please let us know at admin@8bitmooc.org.
