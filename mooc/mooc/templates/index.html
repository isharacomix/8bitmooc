#8bitmooc

Let's program like it's 1985!

Why 8-bit?

Video games have become a ubiquitous part of our culture, but where did they start? Compared to today's games, 8-bit games from the NES, the Atari, and the Gameboy may seem terribly primitive. However, underneath the pixelated sprites and low-resolution sound effects, the devices themselves are very sophisticated, and the programming required to make the iconic games of the era fit in their limited resources was a kind of craftmanship that we don't see in today's games.

Is this a game dev class?

If you're trying to learn how to make games, this is probably not the class for you. We will be intentionally challenging ourselves by developing games using the same platform with the same limitations as NES game developers.

Or, as we like to put it: This course won't teach you how to make Mario - it will teach you how to make Mario the way the people who made Mario made Mario.

Who is this course for?

Anyone interested in the history of technology will find something they can enjoy in this course. If you don't know how to program, this course is a great introduction. If you do, this course strips away the abstractions of higher level programming languages and demonstrates how the hardware implements the programs you write.

No prior programming knowledge is needed to participate in this course.
